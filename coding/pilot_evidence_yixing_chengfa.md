## Why This Case Matters

This case adds a strong Jiangsu county-level city platform and gives a clean
example of formalized project management. The issuer states that it has no land
reserve function, no current land-development or land-repurchase business, no
BT, no project repurchase, no government-purchase-service projects, and no
advance financing for government projects. Yet the same issuer remains one of
Yixing's key infrastructure-construction bodies and performs entrusted
construction and project-management work through fiscal-bureau agreements.

The final label is `functional_transfer`. The evidence points to a shift from
platform borrowing for government projects toward contractual project
management, fiscal settlement, and management-fee compensation.

### Formal Compliance and Negative Functional List

The prospectus states that the issuer does not engage in land-development and
land-reserve functions and that its infrastructure activities are legal and
compliant. It reports no government investment fund, BT, project repurchase,
government-purchase-service project, or government-project advance financing.

1, 2015 is not local government debt.

### Entrusted Construction and Management-Fee Channel

The same prospectus documents a continuing public-project function. The issuer
signs entrusted-construction agreements with the Yixing Finance Bureau, works on
projects assigned by the Yixing government, and receives project payments that
include audited construction costs and management fees. For projects not
formally placed under the issuer, it provides project management and receives a
management fee of three percent of completed investment.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `functional_transfer` at medium confidence.

The issuer's public-development role clearly continues, so the case is not a
substantive exit. At the same time, the documents provide unusually specific
evidence that old platform functions have been formalized through entrusted
construction, project management, fiscal settlement, and management-fee rules.
The issuer remains a public-project manager and infrastructure platform, but
the funding and compensation mechanism is no longer described as informal
government financing.

The main alternative label is `nominal_exit`. The issuer still has large
government-linked receivables, and some other receivables reflect earlier land
demolition and infrastructure-related advances. The final label remains
functional transfer because the current documents explicitly distinguish
project-management and fee-based fiscal settlement from BT, repurchase,
government purchase service, and advance financing for government projects.

Source coverage score: 3. Confidence should rise only after collecting a rating
report or local government document that independently confirms the issuer's
transition from platform financing to fiscal project management.
