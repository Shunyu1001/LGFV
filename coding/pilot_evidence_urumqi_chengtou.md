## Why This Case Matters

This case adds Xinjiang and a western provincial-capital platform to the
validated pool. It is useful because the issuer's own prospectus gives unusually
direct evidence that the company used to function as a government financing
platform while still carrying substantial public-development functions after
the formal compliance point. The case therefore anchors the nominal-exit side of
the typology outside the coastal and central-province samples.

The label is medium confidence. The formal event is based on issuer disclosure,
not an independent platform-list notice. The continued-function evidence is
unusually strong and is corroborated by the rating report.

## Evidence Themes

### No Government Financing Function

The prospectus states that the issuance will not add government debt, will not
involve false debt resolution or new hidden debt, will not be transferred to
government or fiscal use, and will not be directly repaid with fiscal funds. It
also states that the company does not undertake government financing functions
and that new debt after January 1, 2015 is not local government debt. The same
compliance section states that the issuer has no debt directly repaid by fiscal
funds and that the issuance will not add local government debt or hidden debt.

### Infrastructure, Government Purchase Service, PPP, and Receivables

The prospectus states that the issuer is authorized by the Urumqi government as
the project body for urban infrastructure construction. Its infrastructure work
includes roads, bridges, environmental remediation, and other public-service

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `nominal_exit` at medium confidence.

The formal-event evidence comes from the no-government-financing and
post-2015-debt language in the 2026 prospectus. The continued-function evidence
is much stronger than in many other cases. The issuer is still described as
Urumqi's central infrastructure and state-asset platform, and its project
finance remains closely tied to fiscal appropriations, government service
payments, PPP government-pay mechanisms, subsidies, and government receivables.

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. Some projects moved into
government purchase service or PPP channels, and the prospectus says that the
issuer no longer undertakes later-stage investment-financing functions for some
shantytown-renovation projects. The final label remains nominal exit because
the same legal issuer continues to carry a broad set of public development and
financing-related tasks.

Source coverage score: 4. Confidence should rise only if an independent local
government, SASAC, banking-regulator, or platform-list document confirms the
issuer's formal platform-list status or exit event. Historical-capacity metrics
for this added target still need to be merged into the candidate-capacity file.
