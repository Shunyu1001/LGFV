## Coding rationale

This case is coded as `nominal_exit`. The prospectus states that the issuer has
stripped government financing functions, transformed into a self-operating and
self-risk-bearing marketized entity, and does not undertake government
financing functions after January 1, 2015. The legal opinion and prospectus
also repeat the standard no-hidden-debt and no-direct-fiscal-repayment language.

Those formal statements coexist with continuing evidence that the same issuer
is responsible for municipal infrastructure construction, municipal public
projects, affordable housing, land consolidation, and project-related
receivables. The case therefore captures a familiar post-reform pattern:
government financing is denied as a legal status, while the issuer continues to
carry public-development functions through marketized and contractual language.

## Alternative label

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. The documents present
land reserve, hidden debt, and direct fiscal repayment as formally excluded,
and they describe marketized operation after reform. The final label remains
`nominal_exit` because the same issuer continues to carry municipal
infrastructure, public project, affordable housing, and land-consolidation
functions.

## Caveat

The strongest formal-exit language comes from prospectus and legal-opinion
materials rather than a directly collected local government platform-list
document. Confidence should rise if an independent local notice is found.
