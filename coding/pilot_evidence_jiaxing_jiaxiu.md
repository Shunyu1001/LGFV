# Jiaxing Jiaxiu Development Investment Holding Group

Case ID: `sch_20260630_0068`

Company: 嘉兴市嘉秀发展投资控股集团有限公司

Final label: `nominal_exit`

Confidence: `medium`

## Formal event

The 2026 third SCP legal opinion and prospectus contain clean post-2015
compliance language. The legal opinion states that the current issuance will
not increase government debt or hidden debt, will not be transferred to
government or fiscal departments, and will not be directly repaid by fiscal
funds. It also states that the Jiaxing SASAC shareholder bears only limited
liability, that the issuer repays as an independent legal person, that the
company does not undertake government financing functions, and that new debt
after January 1, 2015 is not local-government debt. The prospectus repeats
that the proceeds comply with local-government-debt rules and will not be
directly repaid by fiscal funds.

## Continued function

The same source packet shows continuing public-development functions. The
issuer's legal opinion identifies the company as a state-owned enterprise
created with Jiaxing government approval and controlled by Jiaxing SASAC, with
a business scope that includes infrastructure investment, development, and
construction, land preparation, river dredging, and urban-rural water-pipe
network construction. It says the issuer is an important development,
construction, and state-asset operation body in Xiuzhou District and that its
main businesses include resettlement housing, infrastructure, land
preparation, leasing, trade, agricultural products, machinery sales, kitchen
waste, and real-estate sales. The prospectus records large inventories tied to
entrusted construction projects, large receivables from local government and
state-owned counterparties, recurrent fiscal subsidies, fiscal cash-flow
exposure, and construction activities whose payment depends on Xiuzhou
government or fiscal settlement. It also says resettlement-housing projects
use tripartite entrusted-construction agreements with the Xiuzhou government,
that the issuer and subsidiaries directly finance, invest, and construct the
projects, and that infrastructure construction is performed under entrusted
construction agreements with local governments or state-owned counterparties.

## Coding rationale

The case is coded as nominal exit. The issuer's disclosure package contains
clean no-government-financing and no-hidden-debt language, and the legal
opinion denies several prohibited financing channels. Yet the same issuer
group remains deeply involved in Xiuzhou District's infrastructure,
resettlement-housing, land-preparation, and entrusted-construction functions.
The documents show project financing, government or fiscal settlement,
government-related receivables, fiscal subsidies, and payment risk tied to
local government counterparties. The formal label changed more clearly than
the public-development function.

## Caveat

This is a district-level platform inside Jiaxing rather than a generic
prefecture-level urban construction company. The formal event is based on
prospectus and legal-opinion compliance language rather than an independently
collected administrative platform-list exit notice. The same issuer also has
two earlier June 2026 disclosure packets with consistent language; these are
treated as repeated disclosures rather than separate gold-standard cases.
