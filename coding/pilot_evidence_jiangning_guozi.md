## Why This Case Matters

This is a strong nominal-exit case. The prospectus opens with a formal
statement that the firm has stripped government financing functions and
transformed into a market-oriented entity that bears its own risks. The same
prospectus then shows that the issuer remains Jiangning District's main
infrastructure and state-asset operation body, that project construction is its
dominant revenue source, and that its construction and resettlement-housing
projects rely on entrusted-construction agreements with the science-park
management committee.

The final label is `nominal_exit`. The official category changed, but the
issuer's core development function remains active inside the same corporate
group.

## Evidence Themes

### Formal Exit Language

The prospectus states that the company has stripped government financing
functions and transformed into a market-oriented operating entity that bears its
own risks. Later compliance language repeats that the issuer does not undertake
government financing functions and that new debt after January 1, 2015 is not
local government debt. It also states that government receivables have operating
backgrounds and do not constitute financing for the government.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `nominal_exit` at high confidence.

The evidence is unusually direct. The prospectus supplies explicit formal exit
language, while the same document documents continuing infrastructure
investment, financing pressure, entrusted construction, government-linked
receivables, and cost-plus payment from a management committee. This is not a
substantive exit because the development function remains central to the
issuer's business model. It is also not a clean functional transfer because the
main functions remain inside the issuer's own subsidiaries rather than being
moved to a separate fiscal or asset-management channel.

The alternative label is `functional_transfer`. That reading would become more
plausible if later evidence shows that Jiangning fiscal authorities rather than
the issuer assumed project financing risk after 2015. The current prospectus,
however, still emphasizes issuer-side financing needs, receivables, and
engineering revenue. The nominal label is therefore stronger.

Source coverage score: 4. Confidence could rise further if the original
government or regulator document that recognized the stripping of government
financing functions were collected directly.
