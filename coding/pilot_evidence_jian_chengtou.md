## Coding rationale

This case is coded as `nominal_exit`. The legal opinion states that the Ji'an
government has stripped the issuer's government financing functions and that
the issuer does not borrow for local government or other entities. It also says
that the issue will not increase government or hidden debt and will not be
directly repaid by fiscal funds. But the same issuer remains the city's main
urban infrastructure construction body and continues to operate land
consolidation, infrastructure construction, affordable housing, fiscal
settlement, and government-subsidy functions.

The case is especially useful because the continued-function evidence is not
thin. The packet documents land-consolidation reimbursement by the finance
bureau, entrusted construction, fiscal subsidies, and receivables from the
Ji'an Finance Bureau. This makes it a clear example of formal compliance
coexisting with persistent quasi-fiscal development functions.

## Continued-function evidence

The legal opinion describes the issuer as the main urban infrastructure
construction body. Its business covers land consolidation, infrastructure
construction, and affordable housing. The document explains that land
consolidation costs and proceeds were historically returned through the finance
bureau and that later land-consolidation agreements still involved annual
fiscal payments, financing costs, and management fees.

- `doc_jx_jian_chengtou_2026_cp1_002.txt`, lines 653-683

Project startup funds come from fiscal appropriations and self-raised funds,
and the government settles costs plus a markup. Annual special fiscal subsidies

## Alternative label

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. The documents repeatedly
state legal compliance and frame project work through fiscal settlement rather
than direct government borrowing. The final label remains `nominal_exit`
because land consolidation, entrusted construction, finance-bureau receivables,
and fiscal subsidies remain tied to the same issuer.

## Caveat

is currently supported by the legal opinion, prospectus, and rating disclosures
