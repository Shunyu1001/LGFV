## Why This Case Matters

This case is a useful functional-transfer case from a Jiangsu prefecture-level
subdistrict context. The issuer uses standard formal compliance language after
2015, but the prospectus also gives unusually detailed evidence that public
project functions were reorganized through a local state-asset entity and then
continued through entrusted construction and cost-plus settlement. It therefore
helps show that exit can mean institutional relocation rather than functional
disappearance.

The label is medium confidence. The formal-event evidence comes from issuer
disclosure and finance-bureau confirmation rather than an independent
platform-list notice. The continued-function and transfer evidence is strong
because the prospectus describes the 2018 reorganization, the new settlement
counterparties, and the continuing role of the same issuer.

## Evidence Themes

### No Government Financing Function

The prospectus states that the issuer follows the requirement to strip
government financing functions from financing-platform companies and not add
government debt. It also states that the company does not undertake government
financing functions and that debt newly incurred after January 1, 2015 is not
local government debt. The same section says the issuance does not create new
local-government or hidden debt.

### Reorganized Public-Project Function

The prospectus gives direct evidence of functional transfer. Before 2018, the
issuer was directly entrusted by the district government to carry out land
development, demolition, road construction, old-neighborhood renovation, and
other municipal construction projects. Beginning in 2018, the district
government assigned Taizhou Jiangyan Xingdu Asset Management to manage land
preparation and municipal construction projects. Xingdu then signed entrusted
construction agreements with the issuer, while the issuer continued to handle
capital input, construction, land acquisition, demolition, and project
procedures. Project payments were calculated by construction cost plus entrusted
construction remuneration.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `functional_transfer` at medium confidence.

The formal compliance language is clear, but the empirical pattern is not
simple disappearance. Public-development work was moved into a different
institutional arrangement after 2018. Xingdu Asset Management became the
central management and settlement node, while the issuer continued to supply
capital, organize construction, and receive cost-plus project payments. The
same issuer also remains the key infrastructure and state-asset platform in the
district. The case therefore fits functional transfer better than nominal exit:
the task moved through a new state-asset and delegated-construction channel, but
the fiscal-development function remained active.

The main alternative label is `nominal_exit`. The same issuer remains deeply
involved in public projects and continues to receive government-linked payments
and subsidies, so a narrower interpretation could treat the reform as formal
renaming. The final label remains functional transfer because the prospectus
documents a specific post-2018 restructuring of project management and
settlement authority.

Source coverage score: 4. Confidence should rise only if an independent local
government, SASAC, banking-regulator, or platform-list document confirms the
issuer's formal exit or platform-list status.
