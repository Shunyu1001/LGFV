## Why This Case Matters

This case is a useful boundary case. The issuer was reorganized in 2023 as an
industrial investment holding group, and the documents repeatedly use formal
compliance language about stripping government financing functions. Yet the
same issuer and its consolidated subsidiaries continue to carry functions that
look close to the old LGFV role: traffic infrastructure, land整理, fiscal
payments, government-linked receivables, and project repurchase arrangements.

The final label is `nominal_exit`. The restructuring changed the corporate
label and rearranged assets, but the available documents do not show a clean
transfer of the quasi-fiscal development function out of the issuer's
institutional perimeter.

## Evidence Themes

### Formal Reorganization and New Industrial Holding Label

The legal opinion reports that, in September 2023, Zhenjiang municipal
government issued the notice to establish Zhenjiang Industrial Investment
Holding Group. The company name changed, the shareholder became Zhenjiang SASAC,
and shares in Zhenjiang State-Owned Investment Holding Group and Zhenjiang
Transportation Industry Group were transferred into the issuer. The prospectus
describes the same reorganization as involving core equity transfers, partial
asset stripping, and the injection of higher-quality assets.

### Compliance Language Does Not Eliminate the Function

The same documents contain formal compliance language. The legal opinion says
the issuer does not engage in BT, entrusted construction, land transfer, or
public-welfare operations at the issuer level, and reports that Zhenjiang is
stripping the issuer's government financing function under the post-2014 local
debt regime. It also states that new debt does not increase local government
debt or hidden debt. These claims matter, but they coexist with the continuing
traffic, land, fiscal-payment, and receivables evidence.

### Partial Asset Transfer Without Full Functional Exit

The restructuring is not purely cosmetic. The legal opinion documents asset
transfers after the group was created, including the transfer of rail-transit
equity to Zhenjiang Chengji Commercial Management and the transfer of nine
construction and real-estate-related entities to Zhenjiang Railway Investment
Development. This supports a partial functional-transfer reading, but the same
documents still place transportation and land-development functions within the
issuer's consolidated perimeter.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `nominal_exit` at medium confidence.

The alternative label is `functional_transfer`. The 2023 reorganization and
subsequent asset transfers clearly moved some functions across municipal SOEs.
However, the evidence does not show that the issuer has exited the public
development function in a sustained way. It still controls or consolidates
important vehicles, faces land整理 repayment risk, receives fiscal-linked
support, and remains connected to transportation infrastructure construction
and government repurchase arrangements.

For this reason, the case is more convincing as a nominal exit. The official
language and corporate label changed, but the fiscal-development function
remained active within the same group system.

Source coverage score: 3. Confidence should rise only after collecting the
original 2023 municipal reorganization notice and later disclosures showing
whether the traffic, land整理, and construction entities were fully removed from
the issuer's functional responsibility.
