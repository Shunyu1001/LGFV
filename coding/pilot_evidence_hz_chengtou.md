## Why This Case Matters

Hangzhou is a useful third pilot because it captures a different institutional
pattern from both Xi'an Hi-tech and Guangzhou City Construction Investment.

Xi'an Hi-tech is a `nominal_exit` case: official exit language coexists with
continuing infrastructure financing and project functions. Guangzhou City
Construction Investment is a `substantive_exit` case: old government-project
investment and financing was partly absorbed into formal fiscal channels and
new public projects shifted toward fiscal-funded entrusted management.

Hangzhou instead shows functional consolidation. Public investment and project
functions did not simply disappear. They were reorganized into an enlarged city
investment group through the integration of Qiantou Group, debt succession, and
the creation of Hangzhou Anju Group as the municipal housing-security
investment, construction, and operation platform.

## Evidence Themes

### Group Integration

The prospectus states that in December 2022, under the decisions and deployment
of the municipal party committee and municipal government, Hangzhou City
Investment and Qiantou Group were integrated and reorganized into a new city
investment group. It also describes the creation or elevation of the bus,
energy, and Anju groups, forming a `1+4+X` structure.

Relevant extracted-text location:

- `doc_zj_hz_chengtou_2024_mtn1_prospectus.txt`, lines 1188-1192

### Qiantou Group Equity Transfer

The prospectus states that Hangzhou SASAC transferred 90 percent of Qiantou
Group equity to Hangzhou City Investment as government capital contribution.
After the merger, Hangzhou City Investment held 90 percent of Qiantou Group.

### Debt Succession

The prospectus states that Hangzhou City Investment agreed in principle to
succeed all existing corporate bonds, enterprise bonds, and private-placement
instruments of Qiantou Group, totaling 15.2 billion yuan, and that the
succession had been completed by the date of the prospectus.

### Anju Group Creation

The prospectus states that Hangzhou SASAC approved the creation of Hangzhou
Anju Group as a wholly owned subsidiary of Hangzhou City Investment. It
positions Anju Group as the citywide housing-security investment, construction,

### Continuing Public-Project Functions

The rating report and prospectus show that the enlarged group continues to
perform infrastructure, public-utility, entrusted-construction, land-development,
and PPP functions. The point is not that public functions ended, but that they
were reorganized and consolidated into a new group structure.

## Coding Implication

The final label is `functional_transfer` with medium confidence.

The stronger interpretation is functional consolidation within the
functional-transfer family. The original public-project and debt functions did
not vanish. Qiantou Group's equity and debts were folded into Hangzhou City
Investment, Anju Group was created as a specialized public housing platform, and
the enlarged group continued to operate infrastructure, public-utility,
land-development, and PPP functions.

The main caveat is that some entrusted-construction activity is described as
management-fee based and not direct financing. This means the case should not be
read as pure nominal exit. It is better used to show how a high-capacity city can
adapt by consolidating functions into a more organized municipal platform
system.
