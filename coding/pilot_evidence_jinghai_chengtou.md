This case adds a Tianjin district platform with unusually clear formal-exit
evidence. The prospectus states that Tianjin banking regulators recognized the
issuer as a financing-platform company during the 2010 platform regularization
campaign, but that it later met the platform-loan exit conditions and was
transferred out of the CBRC financing-platform list in June 2011.

legal issuer remained the core local infrastructure, entrusted-construction,
heating, and public-service platform. Its project revenue and receivables remain

## Evidence Themes

### Direct Platform-List Exit Evidence

The prospectus reports that Tianjin banking regulators recognized the issuer as
a financing-platform company under the 2010 regulatory campaign. It then states
that, after creditor banks investigated the issuer's cash flow and marketized
operating conditions, the issuer met the conditions for platform-loan exit and
was transferred out of the CBRC financing-platform list in June 2011.

### Formal Separation from Government Financing

The prospectus and legal opinion state that the issuer does not undertake
government financing functions, that new debt after January 1, 2015 is not local
government debt, and that the current issuance will not add local-government or
hidden debt. The prospectus also says that the issuer has stripped out land
reserve functions and that the current bond will not be directly repaid with
fiscal funds.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `nominal_exit` at high confidence.

The formal event is stronger than in most cases because the prospectus reports
both initial platform recognition and later removal from the CBRC
financing-platform list. Yet the same issuer remains central to local public
investment and service provision. The documents continue to show
infrastructure, BT legacy projects, entrusted construction, fiscal settlement,
government-linked receivables, and public-service support. The official category
changed, but the core local public-development function continued inside the
same corporate shell.

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. Some post-2012 projects
are described as entrusted construction with project funds or management fees
provided by the委托方, and the issuer says it has stripped out land-reserve and
government-financing functions. The final label remains nominal exit because the
issuer itself continues to hold the central project, receivable, fiscal-support,
and public-service position in the local development system.
