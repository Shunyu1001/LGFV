# Codebook for LGFV Exit Types

## Unit of Coding

The preferred unit is a platform company within a prefecture-level city. If
platform-level source materials are incomplete, the unit can be aggregated to
the city level by coding the city's major LGFV or the dominant pattern among its
major platforms.

## Source Materials

Use original or near-original documents whenever possible:

- Local government announcements
- Finance bureau or NDRC notices
- Platform company announcements
- Bond prospectuses
- Credit rating reports
- Annual reports
- Business registration records
- Audit reports
- Public debt management documents
- Credible financial news only as supplementary evidence

## Core Question

After official exit or market-oriented transformation, does the platform still
perform government financing or quasi-fiscal functions?

## Exit Type Categories

### 1. Substantive Exit

Definition: The platform genuinely ceases to perform government financing
functions and no longer acts as an off-budget fiscal arm of the local
government.

Observable indicators:

- Explicit statement that the company no longer undertakes government financing
  functions.
- Business shifts to market-oriented operations with commercial revenue sources.
- No continuing evidence of public welfare project financing, land development
  financing, infrastructure financing, or implicit debt rollover.
- Government project functions are absorbed by formal budgetary channels or
  regulated special-purpose bonds.
- Credit rating or bond documents describe reduced government financing role.

Label as substantive exit only when the evidence supports both formal exit and
functional change.

### 2. Nominal Exit

Definition: The platform is officially removed from a list or declared
transformed, but its core government financing or public project functions
continue with little substantive change.

Observable indicators:

- The company continues to undertake infrastructure construction, land
  development, shantytown redevelopment, public utilities, or public welfare
  projects for the local government.
- Bond documents still emphasize government support, fiscal subsidies, asset
  injections, repurchase arrangements, or project payments.
- The company remains a major local financing platform despite official exit.
- Business scope or debt structure changes only minimally after exit.

### 3. Functional Transfer

Definition: The original platform exits or reduces its role, but its financing,
construction, debt rollover, or project functions are transferred to another
local SOE, subsidiary, merged entity, or newly established quasi-state entity.

Observable indicators:

- Another local SOE begins issuing debt or undertaking projects previously
  associated with the exiting platform.
- Government documents describe asset transfer, merger, reorganization, or
  functional integration.
- A new group company, construction investment company, transportation
  investment company, urban development company, or state capital operation
  company assumes similar functions.
- The original company exits, but local quasi-fiscal financing continues through
  a different organizational vehicle.

### 4. Liquidation

Definition: The company is dissolved, deregistered, liquidated, bankrupted, or
otherwise ceases operations.

Observable indicators:

- Business registration shows cancellation or deregistration.
- Public documents report liquidation, bankruptcy, dissolution, or termination.
- The company no longer issues debt, undertakes projects, or maintains active
  operations.

## Ambiguous Cases

Use `unclear` when documents are insufficient or contradictory. Do not force a
classification when the evidence does not establish the post-exit function.

Common ambiguous situations:

- The document only says "market-oriented transformation" without operational
  details.
- The platform changed its business scope, but available documents do not show
  whether financing functions continued.
- The company exits a government list, but no post-exit documents are available.

## Evidence Standard

Each label should include:

- Final label
- Confidence level: high, medium, or low
- One-sentence rationale
- Short evidence excerpts or source references
- Notes on alternative plausible labels

## Final Coding Rule

LLM labels are preliminary. The final code should be assigned after reviewing
the original source documents and applying this codebook.
