## Why This Case Matters

This case adds a central-China district-level platform from Ganzhou's core
urban district. It is useful because the documents combine formal compliance
language with a dense record of continuing local public-development functions:
municipal construction, shantytown renovation, affordable housing, fiscal
settlement, and transfer-loan arrangements.

The final label is `nominal_exit`. The issuer states that it does not undertake
government financing functions and that new debt after 2015 is not local
government debt. Yet the same issuer remains Zhanggong District's key
construction platform and continues to carry large fiscal and government-linked
project claims.

## Evidence Themes

### Formal Compliance and No-Government-Financing Language

The prospectus states that the issuer does not undertake government financing
functions and that new debt after January 1, 2015 is not local government debt.
It also states that the issuance will not increase government or hidden debt,
will not be transferred to the government or fiscal authorities for use, and
will not be directly repaid by fiscal funds. The legal opinion repeats the
government-financing separation and compliance findings.

### Continuing Construction and Fiscal-Settlement Functions

The prospectus and legal opinion describe the issuer as a construction platform
for municipal projects and affordable-housing projects. The issuer signs
comprehensive development or entrusted-construction agreements with Zhanggong
District government entities, raises funds and invests in construction, and
then applies for settlement. Settlement amounts are reviewed and confirmed by
the Zhanggong Finance Bureau and Zhanggong Guotou, and include construction
costs plus management fees.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `nominal_exit` at medium confidence.

The issuer's formal compliance language is clear, but the underlying public
development function remains attached to the same corporate group. The issuer
continues to raise and deploy project funds, implement municipal and affordable
housing projects, wait for fiscal and state-entity settlement, hold large
government-linked receivables, and participate in shantytown-renovation
transfer-loan arrangements. This is stronger evidence of formal compliance with
continuing function than of substantive institutional exit.

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. Several project flows run
through Zhanggong Guotou, city-level platforms, provincial platforms, and fiscal
settlement channels. The final label remains nominal exit because the same
issuer still retains the central construction, receivable, and project-finance
role in Zhanggong District.

Source coverage score: 4. Confidence should rise only if an independent local
government or regulator document confirms the issuer's formal platform-list
status or exit event.
