# Shijiazhuang Transportation Investment Development

Case ID: `sch_20260630_0151`

Company: 石家庄市交通投资开发有限公司

Final label: `nominal_exit`

Confidence: `medium`

## Formal event

The 2026 prospectus uses the standard post-2015 compliance formula. The issuer
states that the bond proceeds will repay an existing debt-financing instrument,
will not be transferred to government or fiscal departments, and will not be
directly repaid with fiscal funds. The same source states that the shareholder
bears only limited liability, that the issuer repays the debt as an independent
legal person, that the company does not undertake government financing
functions, and that new debt after January 1, 2015 is not local-government
debt. It also reports consultation with the Shijiazhuang Finance Bureau on the
absence of new government or hidden debt.

## Continued function

The same packet shows that the issuer remains embedded in municipal
transport-infrastructure finance. Its revenue depends overwhelmingly on highway
operations, and the prospectus describes the company as responsible for the
planning, construction, operation, and management of major Shijiazhuang highway
assets. Its subsidiaries undertake expressway construction and operation
through PPP and BOT structures. The packet also records government and
shareholder support, including capital injection, state-asset transfer,
government subsidies, project-capital arrangements, concession rights, toll
rights, and pledges over rights to user fees and construction or operating
subsidies.

## Coding rationale

The case is coded as nominal exit. The formal disclosure separates the issuer
from government debt and denies a government financing function, but the
municipal transport-finance function remains inside the issuer group. This is
not a broad land-development platform case. It is a specialized transport
platform case in which formal no-hidden-debt language coexists with continuing
public infrastructure financing, concession, subsidy, and state-asset support.

## Caveat

The formal event is supported by issuer disclosure and finance-bureau
consultation language rather than by an independently collected government
exit-list notice. The label should therefore remain medium confidence until a
separate administrative exit or transformation document is collected.
