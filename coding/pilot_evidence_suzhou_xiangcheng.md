## Coding rationale

This case is coded as `nominal_exit`. The formal disclosure packet uses the
standard post-2015 compliance language: government financing functions have
been stripped, hidden debt should not be increased, bond proceeds should not be
transferred to fiscal authorities, and the government will not directly repay
the issue with fiscal funds. At the same time, the same issuer remains a major
state-owned project investment and construction body for Xiangcheng District.
The documents describe continuing infrastructure construction, resettlement
housing entrusted construction, fiscal settlement, government-linked
receivables, and project cash flows tied to public construction.

The central coding problem is therefore not whether the issuer uses compliant
legal language. It does. The problem is whether the underlying public
development function disappears. The evidence shows continuity through the same
issuer, even though the documents try to normalize that continuity through
contractual and fiscal-settlement language.

The prospectus states that the issuer has orderly stripped government financing
functions, that hidden debt will not be increased, and that identified
government debt will be handled through local-government bond replacement or
other lawful arrangements.

The same packet shows that the issuer continues to perform public-development
functions. The legal opinion reports infrastructure construction and
resettlement-housing entrusted construction. It also explains that fiscal funds
repay commissioned-construction projects each year and that projects are
transferred to the government after acceptance.

## Alternative label

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. The documents emphasize
that the post-reform model excludes PPP, BT, government purchase services,
land reserve, project repurchase, and government advance financing. They also
frame commissioned construction as contractual and fiscally settled. The final
label remains `nominal_exit` because the same issuer continues the public
construction and fiscal-settlement role rather than clearly transferring the
function to a separate budgetary or operating institution.

## Caveat

The packet relies on issuer and legal-opinion disclosures from a 2026 issuance
page. A directly retrieved local government platform-list exit document would
raise confidence.
