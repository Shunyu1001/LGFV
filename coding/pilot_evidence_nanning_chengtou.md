# Nanning City Construction Investment Group

Case ID: `expand_gx_nanning_chengtou`

Company: 南宁城市建设投资集团有限责任公司

Location: Nanning, Guangxi

Final label: `nominal_exit`

Confidence: medium

## Formal event

The 2023 short-term commercial-paper prospectus contains direct legal-compliance language. It states that the issuer's current debt instrument will not increase government debt or hidden local-government debt, that Nanning SASAC bears only limited shareholder liability, and that the company does not undertake government financing functions. The same section states that new debt after August 2018 is not local-government debt.

The source packet also contains a broader post-2015 no-new-local-government-debt formulation. I use 2018 as the case-specific compliance date because the later legal-compliance section gives an issuer-specific statement tied to SASAC consultation.

Primary evidence:

- `doc_gx_nanning_chengtou_2023_cp_prospectus`, lines 5216-5231.

## Continued function evidence

The 2025 rating report shows that the same issuer remains Nanning's most important urban infrastructure construction body. It undertakes urban roads, bridges, municipal key projects, road and bridge tolling, real-estate development, affordable-housing rental, and related state-asset functions. Nanning SASAC remains the sole shareholder and actual controller.

The same report records a partial shift in project financing after 2018. For project-owner designated municipal projects, the company no longer conducts financing and project funds are arranged and disbursed by municipal finance. This is important fiscal-substitution evidence. But the same report also shows that the issuer continues to carry shantytown redevelopment through government purchase service. The company raises bank financing, the housing and urban-rural construction bureau pays service funds from fiscal funds, and these payments are included in the municipal budget and medium- to long-term fiscal plan. At the end of March 2024, 67.28 billion yuan of repayment still depended on future fiscal funding arrangements.

## Coding rationale

This is not a substantive exit because the same issuer continues to house core public-development and fiscal-payment functions after the formal no-government-financing statement. The issuer remains responsible for municipal infrastructure, government-purchase-service shantytown redevelopment, toll-road and bridge assets, affordable-housing rental, government-related receivables, fiscal subsidies, and state-asset support.

The case is also not simply unchanged. The 2018 shift of project-owner designated municipal projects to direct fiscal funding is a real institutional adjustment. I therefore treat it as a nominal-exit case with unusually clear fiscal-substitution evidence rather than as a pure substantive-exit case.

## Alternative label

`substantive_exit`

The alternative label is plausible for the narrow project-owner designated municipal-project segment. The 2025 rating report says that since 2018 the company no longer finances those projects, municipal finance arranges and disburses all project funds, and the issuer receives a management fee. The final label remains nominal because the same issuer still performs other quasi-fiscal public-development functions.

## Caveat

The source packet does not include an independent administrative exit-list notice. The formal event is based on prospectus legal-compliance language and SASAC consultation. The 2023 prospectus also contains both January 2015 and August 2018 versions of the no-new-local-government-debt compliance date; the case file uses 2018 as the issuer-specific event date.
