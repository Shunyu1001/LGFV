# Pilot Evidence Packet: Zunyi Daoqiao

## Case

- Case ID: `pilot_gz_002`

- Former name: 遵义市道路桥梁工程有限责任公司
- Current coding status: candidate evidence packet, not yet human-validated
- Provisional interpretation: likely nominal-exit or debt-continuity case, pending a direct official exit or no-government-financing source

## Why This Case Matters

Zunyi Daoqiao is useful because it adds a weak-capacity inland case to the pilot. The current documents do not yet provide a clean official exit statement, so the case should not be entered into the final label table. They do, however, show the sequence that the paper needs to observe in low-capacity settings: a platform created around infrastructure construction, land development, and local investment-financing; continued post-reform entrusted construction and land-consolidation functions; and a later debt workout that depends on public-sector and financial-institution coordination.

This case should therefore be used as a candidate for testing whether the coding rules can distinguish three things: old platform function, continued public-project function, and formal exit language. The first two are now well documented. The third still needs to be collected.

## Baseline Platform Function

The 2015 prospectus identifies the issuer's subsidiary as the only legal construction entity and investment-financing carrier responsible for New Pu New Area infrastructure projects. The relevant passage states that the subsidiary was approved to meet the development needs of New Pu New Area and served as the construction legal person and investment-financing carrier for infrastructure and public construction projects. The extracted text appears in `doc_gz_zy_daoqiao_2015_bond_prospectus.txt`, lines 943-945.

The same prospectus describes the company as a legal person and investment-financing carrier established for land reserve and consolidation, infrastructure construction, and engineering construction. It also states that the company's operation had become the main body for New Pu New Area urban infrastructure development. The relevant text appears at lines 957-961.

The prospectus also describes a land-infrastructure financing model. New Pu New Area first built urban infrastructure and road networks and then transferred land, with land proceeds and fiscal arrangements supporting infrastructure construction and urban function improvement. Relevant passages appear at lines 999-1004 and 1528-1532.

The risk and rating-summary sections reinforce the same interpretation. The prospectus describes the issuer as a public-welfare infrastructure builder whose assets have limited profitability and notes that the company, as Zunyi's infrastructure investment-financing and construction body, received support through asset injections and government subsidies. These passages appear at lines 1639-1646 and 1662-1664.

## Continued Public-Project Function

The 2022 tracking rating report shows that the platform function had not simply disappeared. The report states that Zunyi Daoqiao's entrusted-construction business was mainly carried out through New Pu Investment, under an agreement with the New Pu New Area Management Committee. The tasks included land acquisition and demolition, pipeline relocation, preliminary procedures, bidding, contract signing, construction management, and final project audit, while the management committee paid project construction funds and management fees. These passages appear in `doc_gz_zy_daoqiao_2022_tracking_rating.txt`, lines 537-543.

The same report states that the land-consolidation business was still carried out by New Pu Investment, that the land-use rights were collected and stored on behalf of the New Pu Management Committee, and that local government departments were expected to replace recovered land certificates with cash, operating assets, land, equity, or concession rights. This evidence appears at lines 613-628.

The 2022 report also records continued public support. It says the company remained an important infrastructure construction body in Zunyi, had undertaken many road, bridge, and municipal projects, and received a 2021 conversion of prior fiscal payments into registered capital. Relevant passages appear at lines 632-636.

## Debt Stress And Restructuring

The 2022 tracking rating report records severe financial stress. It notes that by June 27, 2022, the company had 119 enforcement records, most added since 2021, with an executed amount of RMB 5.236 billion, or 52.36 yi yuan. It also states that the rating outlook remained negative. Relevant passages appear at lines 62 and 131-132.

The 2023 rating attention notice records a bank-loan restructuring. It states that the company had basically clarified restructuring arrangements with banking financial institutions and signed related supplementary agreements. The restructuring involved RMB 15.594 billion, or 155.94 yi yuan, of debt. After restructuring, the bank-loan term was adjusted to 20 years, with rates of 3.00 percent to 4.50 percent per year, interest-only payments in the first 10 years, and amortizing principal repayment in the final 10 years. This evidence appears in `doc_gz_zy_daoqiao_2023_bank_restructuring_notice.txt`, lines 23-28.

The same attention notice records ongoing liquidity pressure. It states that as of January 13, 2023, the company had 158 enforcement records; as of the end of June 2022, total debt was RMB 45.754 billion, or 457.54 yi yuan, short-term debt was RMB 14.141 billion, or 141.41 yi yuan, and the cash-to-short-term-debt ratio was 0.04. It also records RMB 15.146 billion, or 151.46 yi yuan, in external guarantees and says that some nonstandard debt had already become overdue. These passages appear at lines 42-50.

Finally, the notice states that the company still needed support and help from the Guizhou provincial government, Zunyi municipal government, and local financial institutions to improve operating capacity and cash flow and to resolve debt risk. This appears at lines 52-55.

## Coding Implication

The case should not yet be assigned a final label. The current evidence supports three limited claims.

First, Zunyi Daoqiao was clearly a platform-like entity before the current reform period. It combined infrastructure construction, land development, investment-financing, government support, and public-project obligations.

Second, by 2022, the company still carried out entrusted-construction and land-consolidation functions tied to local government arrangements. This is inconsistent with a clean disappearance of public-project functions.

Third, the 2023 bank-loan restructuring suggests that in a weak-capacity, high-debt setting, formal adjustment may take the form of debt maturity extension, fiscal coordination, and continued public-sector support rather than immediate substantive exit.

The remaining missing source is a direct formal-exit or compliance document. The next search should look for documents using the following terms: `遵义道桥 退出融资平台`, `遵义道桥 政府融资职能`, `遵义道桥 不承担政府融资职能`, `遵义道桥 市场化转型`, `遵义道桥 隐性债务 化解`, and `遵义道桥 债务重组 公告`. A final label should be assigned only after this source is found or after the absence of such a source is recorded systematically.
