## Coding rationale

This case is coded as `nominal_exit`. The prospectus and legal opinion contain
clear formal compliance language: the issuer's government financing function
has been stripped, relevant debts have been replaced or otherwise arranged, the
new issue will not increase government or hidden debt, and fiscal funds will not
directly repay the bond. Yet the same source packet continues to describe the
company as a major urban-rural development, infrastructure construction, and
public-resource state-owned asset platform.

The evidence therefore fits the core measurement distinction in the paper:
formal exit does not necessarily imply institutional exit. The official and
legal status of the debt is separated from local government, but the same
issuer continues to perform core public-development functions and remains
linked to government receivables and subsidy channels.

## Alternative label

The main alternative label is `functional_transfer`. The documents stress legal
separation from government debt and describe some receivables as contractual.
The final label remains `nominal_exit` because the same issuer remains central
to the city's public-development system rather than showing a clear transfer of
the function to another fiscal or state-asset channel.
