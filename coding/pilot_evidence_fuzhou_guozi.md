## Why This Case Matters

This case is useful because it separates two forms of exit that are easy to
confuse. The firm did not disappear, and public investment functions did not
vanish from the city. But the legal issuer was reworked from a group whose
original scope included urban infrastructure construction, area development,
public utilities, strategic-industry investment, and real estate into a state
capital investment and operation company. The current documents describe a
company whose direct scope is headquarters management, own-fund investment,
venture investment, consulting, and financing consulting.

The final label is `functional_transfer`. The evidence shows a transfer from
direct local-development functions into a portfolio, fund, and state-capital
operation structure. Infrastructure remains present through equity stakes in
municipal platforms and through the Mindu infrastructure fund, but it no longer
appears as the issuer's own direct urban construction and land-development
function.

## Evidence Themes

### Original Development Mandate and 2022 Scope Change

The legal opinion gives a clear before-and-after sequence. The firm was set up
in 2013 as Fuzhou State-Owned Assets Investment Development Co., Ltd. Its
original business scope included urban infrastructure construction, key-area
and district comprehensive development, public utilities, environmental and gas
services, livelihood projects, urban energy, automotive culture, development,
operation, and real estate. In January 2022, the Fuzhou SASAC approved the
company's name change to Fuzhou State Capital Investment and Operation Group
and changed its business scope to headquarters management, own-fund investment,
venture investment, information consulting, socioeconomic consulting, and
financing consulting.

### Portfolio Reorganization Rather Than Disappearance

The prospectus reports major 2022 asset reorganizations. The issuer transferred
out wholly owned interests in water, construction, market-supply, tea, garden
development, and wind-related entities, while receiving equity stakes in a set
of municipal state-owned groups. The second restructuring brought in 20 percent
stakes in Fuzhou Chengtou, Fuzhou Zuohai, Fuzhou Metro, Fuzhou New Area
Development Investment, and other municipal companies. This points to a change
in institutional position rather than liquidation.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `functional_transfer` at medium confidence.

It is not a substantive exit because infrastructure and public investment
functions remain visible through the issuer's equity portfolio and through the
Mindu infrastructure fund. The company also remains a strategically important
municipal state-capital vehicle with strong government support. At the same
time, it is not simply a nominal exit. The same legal issuer's formal business
scope and organizational role changed sharply in 2022. Direct urban
infrastructure construction and area development moved out of the issuer's own
mandate and into separate municipal platforms, equity stakes, and fund
structures.

The main alternative label is `substantive_exit`. If the coding rule focuses
only on the issuer's direct operating scope, the disappearance of direct
infrastructure and real-estate development from the issuer's business scope
looks close to substantive exit. The final label remains functional transfer
because the evidence shows that infrastructure did not disappear from the
issuer's institutional orbit. It was reorganized into portfolio ownership and
fund-based investment.
