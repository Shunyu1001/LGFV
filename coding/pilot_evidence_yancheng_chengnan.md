## Why This Case Matters

This case is useful because it contains both a careful legal-compliance
negative list and clear evidence that platform-like development functions
continue. The legal opinion states that the issuer does not undertake
government financing functions, that post-2015 new debt is not local-government
debt, that the proceeds will not increase hidden debt, and that the issuer has
no PPP, government investment fund, BT, or project-repurchase activity.

At the same time, the same source packet shows that the issuer remains the most
important investment-financing body for Yannan High-tech Zone, with land
development, infrastructure construction, government entrusted construction,
and project repayment through fiscal settlement. This is therefore a useful
case for separating formal legal compliance from actual functional exit.

## Evidence Themes

### Formal Compliance and Negative List

The legal opinion states that the issue will not increase government or hidden
debt, will not be transferred to the government or finance department, and will
not be repaid directly by fiscal funds. It also states that the company does
not undertake government financing functions and that new debt after January 1,
2015 is not local-government debt. Later, the legal opinion says the issuer
does not have PPP projects, government investment funds, BT, project repurchase,
or government receivables without a normal business background.

## Validation Decision

The case is coded as `nominal_exit` at medium confidence.

It is not substantive exit because the issuer remains deeply tied to land
development, infrastructure construction, government entrusted construction,
finance-bureau project settlement, and infrastructure cash-flow cycles. It is
not functional transfer because the quasi-fiscal development functions remain
inside the issuer rather than being clearly moved to a different platform or
absorbed into a fully budgetary mechanism.

The alternative label is `functional_transfer`. That interpretation would
become more plausible if later evidence shows that legal compliance coincided
with a real shift of project financing risk away from the issuer. The current
evidence points instead to formal normalization without functional exit.
