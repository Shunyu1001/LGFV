# Nanjing Transportation Construction Investment Holding

Case ID: `sch_20260630_0052`

Company: 南京市交通建设投资控股(集团)有限责任公司

Final label: `nominal_exit`

Confidence: `medium`

## Formal event

The 2026 MTN prospectus uses the standard post-2015 compliance language. The
issuer promises that proceeds will support normal production and operation, will
not increase government debt or hidden debt, will not be transferred to
government or fiscal departments, and will not be directly repaid with fiscal
funds. The prospectus also states that the issuer does not undertake government
financing functions and that new debt after January 1, 2015 is not
local-government debt. The legal opinion repeats the same position and adds
that the issuer does not use reserve land, government repurchase, BT, PPP,
government funds, or fiscal funds as illegal financing support for this issue.

## Continued function

The same source packet shows that the issuer remains central to Nanjing's
transport infrastructure system. The prospectus describes the group as funded
by Nanjing SASAC and as the main organizer and implementation body for urban
transport construction, road and bridge construction, and road and bridge
operation. It also records municipal capital increases, SASAC transfers,
large-scale road, bridge, expressway, railway-supporting, and station-supporting
projects, debt pressures connected to transport infrastructure investment,
government-related receivables, toll-right pledges, public-institution
counterparties, fiscal subsidies, and subsidiary groups organized around
highways, railway construction, transport land development, and transport
project management.

## Coding rationale

The case is coded as nominal exit. The formal documents draw a clean legal line
between the issuer and local-government debt, but the same issuer group
continues to house the city's transport-infrastructure investment,
construction, operation, financing, asset, toll-right, and fiscal-support
functions. This is not evidence that the old LGFV function disappeared. It is
evidence that a specialized municipal transport platform can combine compliant
no-government-financing language with continuing public infrastructure finance.

## Caveat

The case is a specialized transport-infrastructure group rather than a generic
urban construction platform. It is included because the documents show a
platform-like local public-investment and financing role. The formal event is
based on prospectus and legal-opinion compliance language rather than an
independently collected administrative exit-list notice. The label should
therefore remain medium confidence until a separate regulatory or municipal
exit document is collected.
