\title{Beyond Official Exit: Historical State Capacity and the Institutional Types of LGFV Exit Reform in China}
\author{Shunyu Hao}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
China's local government financing vehicle (LGFV) exit reform is commonly
described as a process through which platform companies withdraw from government
financing functions. Yet official exit does not necessarily imply substantive
exit. Some platform companies genuinely cease performing quasi-fiscal financing
functions, while others remain active under new labels, transfer their functions
to other local state-owned entities, or disappear through liquidation. This paper
argues that the type of LGFV exit varies systematically with local state
capacity. Localities with stronger historically rooted administrative and fiscal
capacity are more likely to achieve substantive exit because they can absorb
former platform functions through formal fiscal instruments and budgetary
management. Localities with weaker state capacity are more likely to display
nominal exit or functional transfer. The paper develops a new coding scheme for
LGFV exit types and uses LLM-assisted, human-validated coding of government
announcements, company disclosures, bond prospectuses, credit rating reports,
and business registration records to distinguish substantive exit, nominal exit,
functional transfer, and liquidation.
\end{abstract}
