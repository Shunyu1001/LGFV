\title{Beyond Official Exit: Historical State Capacity and the Institutional Types of LGFV Exit Reform in China}
\author{Shunyu Hao}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
China's local government financing vehicle (LGFV) exit reform is usually
described as a process through which platform companies withdraw from
government financing functions. Yet official exit does not necessarily imply
substantive exit. Some platforms cease performing quasi-fiscal financing
functions, while others continue under new labels, transfer their functions to
other local state-owned entities, or disappear through liquidation. This paper
argues that LGFV exit should be measured as an institutional type rather than a
binary event. Localities with stronger historically rooted administrative and
fiscal capacity are more likely to achieve substantive exit because they can
absorb former platform functions through formal fiscal instruments, budgetary
management, and state asset reorganization. Localities with weaker capacity are
more likely to display nominal exit or functional transfer. The paper develops a
new coding scheme for LGFV exit types and uses LLM-assisted, human-validated
coding of government documents, company disclosures, bond prospectuses, credit
rating reports, legal opinions, and business registration records to distinguish
substantive exit, nominal exit, functional transfer, and liquidation.
\end{abstract}
