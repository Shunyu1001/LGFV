\section{Conclusion}

LGFV exit reform is not only a debt management policy. It is a test of whether
the central state can formalize local fiscal behavior after years of reliance on
quasi-fiscal instruments. This paper argues that the meaning of exit varies
across localities. Some cities achieve substantive exit, while others display
nominal compliance or transfer financing functions to new entities.

By disaggregating LGFV exit into institutional types, the paper shifts attention
from whether localities exited to what kind of exit they achieved. This
distinction provides a sharper way to study center-local relations, fiscal
formalization, and historical persistence in contemporary Chinese political
economy.
