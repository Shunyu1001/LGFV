\section{Coding Strategy}

The measurement contribution of the paper is a new coding scheme for LGFV exit
types. For each platform, I collect original materials including local
government announcements, platform company disclosures, bond prospectuses,
credit rating reports, business registration records, and relevant public
documents. The coding process evaluates whether the company continues to perform
government financing functions, infrastructure construction, land development,
public project financing, debt rollover, or other quasi-fiscal activities after
its official exit or transformation.

The coding workflow is LLM-assisted and human-validated. First, I define all
exit types in a fixed codebook and specify observable decision rules. Second, I
ask large language models to generate preliminary classifications using the same
codebook and source excerpts. Third, the models must provide textual evidence
for each classification. Fourth, cases with model disagreement, low confidence,
or insufficient evidence are manually reviewed. The final label is assigned by
the researcher based on the original documents, not by the model output alone.

This design treats large language models as auxiliary coding tools rather than
as final decision makers. The purpose is to increase coding efficiency and
transparency while preserving the interpretive judgment required for historical
and institutional classification.
