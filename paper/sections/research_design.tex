\section{Research Design}

The unit of analysis is a city-platform case. By a city-platform case, I mean a
major LGFV or platform-like local state-owned enterprise located within a
prefecture-level city. This unit is narrower than a city-year measure of exit,
but it is better suited to the measurement problem in the paper. Official
statistics usually report whether a locality has reduced the number of
platforms or the volume of platform debt. The coding task here is different: it
asks where the financing, construction, project-management, and debt functions
associated with a particular platform went after an official exit,
transformation, or reorganization event.

The dependent variable is LGFV exit type. I code each case into one of four
categories: substantive exit, nominal exit, functional transfer, or liquidation.
The first stage of the project uses a pilot sample rather than a large
cross-sectional dataset. This choice is intentional. The categories can only be
used in a larger sample if they can first be applied consistently to original
documents in cases with different institutional patterns. The pilot therefore
prioritizes variation in city capacity, platform function, and document quality
over representativeness.

The current pilot contains four human-validated cases. Xi'an Hi-tech Holding is
coded as nominal exit because issuer disclosures state that the company exited
the financing-platform list in 2012, while bond and rating documents continue
to describe infrastructure investment, BT, entrusted-construction, and
government-linked repayment arrangements. Chengdu City Construction Investment
Management Group is also coded as nominal exit, but for a different documentary
reason: the prospectus states that the company does not undertake government
financing functions after 2015, while the same document describes continuing
infrastructure investment, self-funded and financed project construction,
fiscal payment arrangements, and local debt replacement. Guangzhou City
Construction Investment Group is coded as substantive exit because older and
current disclosures state that, after 2014, the company no longer participated
in new government-project investment and financing, shifted to fiscal-funded
entrusted management, and had part of an earlier MTN absorbed into the fiscal
budget through government debt replacement. Hangzhou City Investment Group is
coded as functional transfer because public project and debt functions were
consolidated into an enlarged municipal platform system through the integration
of Qiantou Group, debt succession, and the creation of Hangzhou Anju Group.
These cases do not prove the historical state capacity argument. They validate
the measurement scheme and clarify the documentary distinctions that the larger
sample must preserve.

The main explanatory variable is historical state capacity. Candidate measures
include Qing-dynasty county and prefecture density, distance to historical
administrative centers, imperial examination degree density, historical tax
capacity, historical transportation infrastructure, courier station density, and
other indicators of historical administrative penetration.

Contemporary controls include GDP per capita, fiscal self-sufficiency, local
debt burden, land finance dependence, industrial structure, population size,
marketization, and political tenure. These variables help distinguish the
historical state capacity argument from purely contemporary fiscal and economic
conditions.
