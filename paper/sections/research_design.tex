\section{Research Design}

The unit of analysis is a city-platform case. By a city-platform case, I mean a
major LGFV or platform-like local state-owned enterprise located within a
prefecture-level city. This unit is narrower than a city-year measure of exit,
but it is better suited to the measurement problem in the paper. Official
statistics usually report whether a locality has reduced the number of
platforms or the volume of platform debt. The coding task here is different: it
asks where the financing, construction, project-management, and debt functions
associated with a particular platform went after an official exit,
transformation, or reorganization event.

The dependent variable is LGFV exit type. I code each case into one of four
categories: substantive exit, nominal exit, functional transfer, or liquidation.
The first stage of the project uses a pilot sample rather than a large
cross-sectional dataset. This choice is intentional. The categories can only be
used in a larger sample if they can first be applied consistently to original
documents in cases with different institutional patterns. The pilot therefore
prioritizes variation in city capacity, platform function, and document quality
over representativeness.

The current pilot contains five human-validated cases. These cases do not
prove the historical state capacity argument. They validate the measurement
scheme and clarify the documentary distinctions that the larger sample must
preserve. The cases fall into three groups. First, Guangzhou City Construction
Investment Group is coded as substantive exit because older and current
disclosures state that, after 2014, the company no longer participated in new
government-project investment and financing, shifted to fiscal-funded entrusted
management, and had part of an earlier MTN absorbed into the fiscal budget
through government debt replacement. Second, Hangzhou City Investment Group is
coded as functional transfer because public project and debt functions were
consolidated into an enlarged municipal platform system through the integration
of Qiantou Group, debt succession, and the creation of Hangzhou Anju Group.
Third, Xi'an Hi-tech Holding, Chengdu City Construction Investment Management
Group, and Zhuzhou State-owned Assets Investment Holding Group are coded as
nominal exit. In Xi'an, issuer disclosures state that the company exited the
financing-platform list in 2012, while bond and rating documents continue to
describe infrastructure investment, BT, entrusted-construction, and
government-linked repayment arrangements. In Chengdu, the prospectus states
that the company does not undertake government financing functions after 2015,
while the same document describes continuing infrastructure investment,
self-funded and financed project construction, fiscal payment arrangements, and
local debt replacement. In Zhuzhou, the prospectus reports a March 2013 exit
from local government financing platform management, while the same source set
documents continuing land preparation, major project construction,
government-entrusted work, fiscal settlement, subsidies, and local government
support.

The pilot also includes candidate cases that are not yet human-validated. These
cases are useful because they show where the coding scheme still needs more
source material before a final label can be assigned. Luzhou Xinglu is the
closest such case. Its 2015, 2017, 2018, 2021, and 2023 source sets show a
continuous pattern of infrastructure investment-financing, government-entrusted
construction, fiscal receivables, project repayment, and government support.
They also contain no-government-financing and post-2015 local-debt-compliance
language. The case is not promoted to a final label because the project has not
yet found a direct platform-list exit or debt-resolution source that names
Xinglu or the Luzhou platform cluster. The current Guizhou candidate cases,
Zunyi Daoqiao and Liupanshui Kaitou, show a related problem. Both companies
have documents establishing baseline platform functions, and both continue to
appear in later rating materials as important public-project or
infrastructure-construction bodies under conditions of fiscal and debt
pressure. These source patterns are valuable for the research design because
they show that weak-capacity cases may appear in the documentary record through
debt-resolution, rating-pressure, and restructuring files rather than through
clean compliance language.

The main explanatory variable is historical state capacity. Candidate measures
include Qing-dynasty county and prefecture density, distance to historical
administrative centers, imperial examination degree density, historical tax
capacity, historical transportation infrastructure, courier station density, and
other indicators of historical administrative penetration.

Contemporary controls include GDP per capita, fiscal self-sufficiency, local
debt burden, land finance dependence, industrial structure, population size,
marketization, and political tenure. These variables help distinguish the
historical state capacity argument from purely contemporary fiscal and economic
conditions.
